About the Project

Understanding Our Mission

Our Aim

Our aim to be corrected from the word "while": While increasing students' access to quality learning resources through donations and kit distribution.

Project outcome

We conducted 3 teaching sessions, at CDG Wahdat Colony, Jeevanhana, Dar ul ilm CPC Care school. We created our own organization named Umeed-e-nau and raised PKR60k for donations to these underprivileged schools and donated at foundations like the alkhidmat Foundation. We also signed successful collaborations with the akhidmat foundation and Punjab Library Foundation along with 150+ resource kits distributed featuring stationery and books.

The Problem

📚
Lack of Quality Resources

Underprivileged students often lack access to updated textbooks, stationery, and meaningful educational materials, creating persistent learning disadvantages.

📉
Impact on Students

Without structured materials, students fall behind, lose motivation, and are excluded from opportunities available to their more privileged peers.

Why This Matters Globally and Locally: According to UNESCO, over 244 million children worldwide are still out of school, with educational inequality disproportionately affecting low-income communities. In Pakistan, millions of children lack access to even basic learning materials — a crisis that directly stunts economic mobility and perpetuates cycles of poverty. Project Mindshift addresses these gaps head-on, starting locally but aspiring to model scalable change.

Research & Key Findings

What We Discovered

Based on 200+ survey responses and in-depth community interviews, our research uncovered critical barriers to learning.

01

Quality Gaps in Resources

Around 80% of the students reported that one of the most significant barriers to their educational progress is the lack of access to high-quality learning resources, indicating that inadequate study materials, outdated content, and limited academic support are severely impacting their ability to learn effectively and achieve their full potential.

Poor quality materials78%
Lacking core textbooks65%
Source: Student Survey (n=200+)
02

Learning Environment Impact

Interviews revealed that 71% of students studied in environments with poor lighting, overcrowded classrooms, or no dedicated study space — compounding the resource gap.

Poor study environment71%
Overcrowded classrooms58%
Source: Community Interviews
03

Books as Primary Barrier

Approximately 83% of the students indicated that the main factors hindering their learning are the high cost and poor quality of available educational resources, particularly books, which are often either too expensive to afford or lack the depth, clarity, and updated content necessary for effective learning.

Cannot afford books83%
Dropouts due to cost42%
Source: Survey + Interviews

Umeed-e-Nau is a student-led initiative established as part of our project to address educational inequality by improving access to quality learning resources. It focuses on empowering underprivileged students through awareness, resource distribution, and collaborative efforts with institutions. Project Evaluation

Short-term impact achieved, long-term impact requires scaling. While Project Mindshift successfully delivered measurable improvements in student motivation and resource access, sustaining and expanding this impact requires continued funding, policy advocacy, and broader institutional partnerships.

Policy Proposals

Our Proposed Solutions

Formal policy recommendations to address systemic educational inequity at scale.

I

Standardisation of Learning Resources

Government bodies should mandate uniform quality standards for educational materials distributed to public schools, ensuring that all students — regardless of socioeconomic background — receive equivalent resources.

Regulation
II

Teacher Involvement & Training

Educators must be equipped with modern pedagogical tools and mandated to actively identify and support resource-deprived students. Teacher training programmes should include equity-focused frameworks.

Education Reform
III

Public-Private Partnerships

Encourage structured collaboration between government institutions, NGOs, and the private sector to co-fund and co-implement sustainable educational resource programmes at national scale.
